autoKIDD

Independent Transportation for Kids

Project Overview

THE PROBLEM

One of the most significant challenges of having children in the modern world is the careful planning of appointments, activities and events - making sure your children are on time for school and extra curricular activities. For many families the hours spent on weekly child transportation logistics are the equivalent of a full working day.

THE SOLUTION

autoKIDD is an autonomous transportation service for children. Parents are able to book or schedule supervised rides and monitor trip progress. By using innovative autonomous technology autoKIDD makes transporting people safe, enjoyable and seamless - ensuring the requester is in control - and passengers are carefree.

User Research 

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

  • To investigate how families transport children to school and extra-curricular  activities
  • To identify difficulties involved in current processes



RESEARCH METHODS

  • Public Survey
  • Interviews
  • Contextual Observations

INTERVIEWS

In order to gather concrete information about existing experiences and to develop empathy with the user, I conducted interviews with four participants who represented the target audience of primary school parents.

KEY INSIGHTS

Time pressure is an ever present issue for parents when transporting or arranging transport of their children to school and other activities.
Were a parent to allow a child to be transported in an autonomous vehicle, a supervisor is preferred for young children, and various forms of communication should be easily available - talk, video and chat.
School buses have a downside for many children and parents - they are not protected from systematic bullying - which can go on for long periods without parents knowing.
A frequently used service needs to be easy to use and able to afford flexibility with changing circumstances.

DATA ANALYSIS

In order to derive useful insights about the design problem, all data gathered from public survey, interviews and contextual observations were analysed and synthesised through an affinity diagram. Several themes emerged from this process which were used in the development of personas and storyboards.

RESEARCH FINDINGS

  • There is a need to improve high quality options for transport
  • A high level of supervision is required for primary age children by parents
  • Surveillance - A tool to directly monitor the child, including location, images, etc. This would add some emotional comfort, but also provide some safety benefit.
  • Human connection is generally valued higher than convenience. Solutions must fit into the parent’s current routine.

PERSONAS

Based on the insights and key findings generated from the research, I developed two personas. Designing for Belinda and Paige helped in making informed design decisions as I had the end user always in mind.

Design Process

CONCEPT GENERATION

After putting all our research findings together and brainstorming design ideas, our team generated two main concepts:

  • The requester app. for parents
  • The passenger app. for children

We developed some lo-fi wireframes in POP and tested them with potential users. We then evaluated all gathered data and came up with our refined concept. 

autoKIDD 

An app. for parents/guardians to request an autonomous vehicle to transport their children to and from school and other activities.

SKETCHES

We used sketching to explore a broad range of ideas and to effectively communicate several interface solutions together. Through over 80 different versions and several iterations we explored each task’s usability and the flow between screens. 

STORYBOARD

We developed a storyboard to show the user’s behavioral flow from ordering a ride to successfully welcoming her child at home with autoKIDD integrated in her behavioral decision making. These flows helped in defining the necessary features required for user to successfully navigate through the app.

LOW FIDELITY PAPER PROTOTYPE

Using our final iteration of sketches and defining user flows, we built the low fidelity prototype in POP.

Testing, Evaluation, Iteration

EVALUATION STAGE 1

We conducted some usability tests where we observed how users complete tasks and where they encounter experience confusion. The aim of these tests were to gauge the flow of screens and to ensure the UI makes sense on a holistic level before progressing on to features.

KEY FINDINGS

  • Availability and Price are important to show when users are making decisions to book a new trip
  • The Customise options need some clarification around their details, and how this affects the trip
  • The Map UI, Trip Status, ETA and Arrival Time are key information

WIREFRAMES

Based on key finding from the first evaluation we moved onto Sketch for wireframing. Keeping the general flow of screens, we worked towards  cleaning up the interactions, adding more  details to each screen and increasing the fidelity slightly to produce a more realistic prototype. 

INTERACTIVE PROTOTYPE

Using our final iteration of wireframes and user flows, we built an interactive low fidelity prototype in InVision and took it to second stage of testing.

EVALUATION STAGE 2

In order to evaluate the interactive prototype, we conducted more usability tests. This time the focus was to look at user flows and also test in alternative settings. Our main concern were assessing what different number of participants could achieve easily or not so and their thoughts about the model of flow.

KEY FINDINGS

  • Ease and speed of use especially when booking a ride
  • More flexibility around customise options
  • The Map UI, Trip Status, Arrival Times should be easily accessible

INTERACTIVE HI-FI PROTOTYPE

Based on the second evaluation results we moved to more high fidelity mockups. Through several iterations we explored different aspects of interface design like colors, shapes, layout, and typefaces (the "look"), as well as the behavior of dynamic elements such as buttons, boxes, and menus (the "feel"). The final interactive prototype was made in InVision. 

KEY LEARNINGS

Taking the time to empathise with users helps with understanding and identifying the context, emotions, goals, and motivations that affects a user’s interaction with a product. We were able to implement some dynamic features in our final product which have arisen from sound methodology and careful listening to our participants, as well as observing their engagement.
